In the book The Distracted Mind: Ancient Brains in a High-Tech World, Adam Gazzaley & Larry D. Rosen point out that with the ultra fast emergence of new technologies requiring rapid responses to emails, texts & working on multiple projects simultaneously.


Our brains have become optimized for rapid task switching, especially in the younger population.


As a result, we all are having greater problems with sustaining attention & we easily become distracted.


Recently, a team from the Behavioural & Clinical Neuroscience Institute at the University of Cambridge, have developed a simple game that is aimed at helping users drastically improve their cognition, memory & concerntration.


In a study published in the journal Frontiers in Behavioural Neuroscience, Professor Sahakian & colleague Dr George Savulich have demonstrated that playing this game for around 10-15 minutes a day, greatly improves attention & concentration, with noticeable effects after just one month.


In this study, the researchers divided 75 healthy young adults into three groups: one group played Decoder, one control group played Bingo for the same amount of time & another control group didnt play either game.


All 75 participants were tested at the start of the trial & then after four weeks using the CANTAB Rapid Visual Information Processing test.


Results from the study showed a significant difference in attention & cognition as measured by the RVP. Those who played this game scored significantly better than those who played Bingo & those who played no games at all.
